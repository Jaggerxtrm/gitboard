# Session Report ‚Äî 2026-05-22

## Summary

Session began with deploying gitboard from a fresh VPS clone (forge-qa5g) and ended six PRs later with an architectural audit-first epic (forge-kcxa). Every PR fixed a real bug; every PR's smoke-test surfaced another. The cumulative pattern revealed the underlying problem: the dashboard has no client-side source-of-truth layer ‚Äî every hook reinvents subscribe + cache + invalidation + refetch independently, and the server-side publishers have drifted in their event-shape and freshness semantics.

The session's most impactful discovery was the **WS subscribe contract mismatch** (forge-7cyq): `WsClient.subscribe` sent `{type:"subscribe", ...}` while the server `handleMessage` routes on `msg.action`. Every realtime channel subscription from the dashboard had been silently dropped for an unknown duration; the dashboard's "live updates" were entirely HTTP-polling-driven. Fixing this exposed a latent event storm (forge-h830) that had been buffered to dead subscribers and was now actually delivered to clients, causing the UI to thrash.

End-state: dashboard is push-driven and stable on a stable system; first-paint latency and observability-registry scope are known residuals scoped under the new audit epic.
